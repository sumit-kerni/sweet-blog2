Jammu and Kashmir

Jammu & Kashmir boasts being flavoursome and diverse.The union territory with its variety of dishess makes an excellent destination for cullinary tourism and attracts food lovers from around the world.The food of Jammu & Kashmir can be divided int two- Jammu Cuisine and Kashmiri Cuisine;each of which is unique in their way.In Jammu, the dishes of Dogra cuisine are quite famous.The beautiful valley of Kashmir reflects a rich influence of Persian, Central Asian and Afghan food in its cuisine

Chocolate Barfi

Chocolate Barfi is an extermely delectable sweet recipie with no chocolate in it.Yes, you read it right.There's no chocolate in the recipe but only added in the name. Don't know the history behind naming it(jammu) chocolate but once you put a piece of this yummiest jammu chocolate in your mouth,you will be mesmerized by its taste and least interested in knowing the history behind.Lol.Jammu chocolate is such a sweet having taste and texture different from other sweet recipes.

Kashmiri Shufta

Shufta is made with lots of dry fruits and sugar.This desert is widely preferred during winters because of its high quantity of dry fruits that helps to wade off chillness in winters. This is one of the traditional delicacies of kashmiri cuisine.

Kesar Phirni

Phirni is a popular dessert of Kashmir.It is made up of rice,saffron,milk,cardamom,etc.Dry fruits and rose essence are also added to give it a special flavour.The dessert gets its real flavour or taste with which the rice is ground.They are served in traditional earthen bowls called 'shikoras'.Other states have also started making this dessert for special occasion.

Baqerkhani

Baqerkhani is a kashmiri rough puff pastry.It forms an intergal part of kashmiri cuisine.The Kashmiri Baqerkhani is usally soft as compared to the middle east variety.It is usually a round naan,which is crisp and sprinkled with sesame seeds.It is usally served as breakfast.

Tosha

Tosha an age-old Kashmiri dessert,has its roots in North Kashmir.Its history is drawn in the sufi roots of Kashmir valley.Tosha is usually made as a symbol of respect on the occasion of joy or grief and has religious values and sentiments attached to it. Tosha is made of flour with ghee,poppy seeds and dry fruits used to give a unique flavour to the taste buds. In North Kashmir,it is prepared and distributed among neighbours and relatives on special occasions.
