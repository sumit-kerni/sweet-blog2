Telangana

The Telugu states of Telangana have a rich variety of desserts which are specially prepared and savoured during festivals like Sankranti, Dasara, Diwali and other celebratory occasions.

Qubani ka meetha

This sweet dish is very popular in telangana that is prepared from dried apricoats, rich quantity of desi ghee, almonds and gulab jal. Once you taste, you cannot forget the amazing aroma and the taste of Khubani.

Shahi tukda

Shahi tukda is a Mughlai dessert made with ghee fried bread, thickened sweetened milk, saffron and nuts. Shahi is a Persian word meaning Royal and tukda or tukra is a hindi & urdu term meaning a piece. Shahi tukda literally translates to a royal piece of dessert.

Firni

Phirni also called as firni is a classic slow-cooked Indian sweet pudding made with basmati rice, milk, nuts, sugar and scented with cardamom powder or saffron or rose water. It is a must in North India on festive occasions or special celebrations like Diwali as well as for Karwa Chauth Festival.

Badam ki jaali

Indulge in the rich, sweet goodness of badam ki jali - a traditional sweet hailing from the city of Hyderabad. Made with creamy almonds and a blend of spices, this treat is sure to satisfy your sweet tooth and leave you wanting more.

Nankhatai

Who doesn’t love Nankhatai? Nankhatai is popular since Nizam’s period and have strong root in Hyderabad. It is a crispy biscuit prepared with bread that is loved by children to adults.
